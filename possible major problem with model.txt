• Best overall long-term fix is: switch to a two-stage realtime pipeline on top of timestamp-aligned data.

  1. Fix the data foundation first (highest priority)
     Resample all channels by timestamp to one common rate (e.g., 2000 Hz) in both training and realtime.
     If train and realtime are not time-aligned the same way, neutral overprediction will keep happening.
  2. Use a 2-stage model instead of one flat 6-class model
     Stage A: neutral vs active (binary).
     Stage B: classify only active gestures (left_turn/right_turn/...).
     This prevents neutral from absorbing everything and scales much better when you re-add all gestures.
  3. Handle transition windows explicitly
     Keep low-confidence transition windows out of gesture training (or label as unknown/transition reject).
     Most neutral bias comes from ambiguous boundary windows.
  4. Replace single global confidence gate with temporal hysteresis
     Enter gesture with higher threshold, exit with lower threshold (state machine), instead of pure moving average +
     one threshold.
     This reduces flicker and neutral fallback.
  5. Tune for recall targets, not just accuracy
     Select models by macro recall / per-class recall floors (especially left/right), not only total accuracy.
  6. Use class-sensitive loss for long-term stability
     Weighted focal/cost-sensitive loss so missed turns are penalized more than extra neutral predictions.

  If you want one “big” fix that most directly solves your current issue and still works when all gestures return: (1)
  timestamp resampling consistency + (2) two-stage neutral/active architecture + hysteresis decoder.
  That is the highest-leverage path.
